The “Don’t Accidentally Start a Fire” Checklist

Run your post through this before publishing:

1. 🔥 Absolutism check 

Scan for:

“always”
“never”
“they are”
“this is”
Replace with:
“often”
“can”
“tends to”
“in some cases”

👉 Rule:

If someone can read it and think “that’s not me”, you’re safer.

2. 🎯 “Am I describing or judging?”

Ask:

Does this sound like an observation… or a verdict?

Risky:

“They miss the bigger picture”

Safe:

“This can make it harder to see the bigger picture”

👉 Shift from person → pattern

3. 🪞 “Would someone feel called out?”

This is the big one.

Ask:

Could a specific person read this and think “this is about me”?

If yes:

add ambiguity
generalise
include yourself
Your good move:

“myself included at times”

That line alone reduces defensiveness massively.

4. ⚖️ “Have I respected both sides?”

Even if you favour one:

show where the other approach is valuable
explicitly acknowledge its strengths

👉 You did this really well here:

“super valuable when this is the case”

This is what stops it sounding like a takedown.

5. 🧠 “Am I attacking identity or context?”
Dangerous:

“These people are…”

Safe:

“In certain situations, this approach…”

👉 Always make it situational, not personal.

6. 🧩 “Is this falsifiable?”

If someone can argue:

“No, that’s just wrong”

…it creates conflict.

If instead they think:

“Hmm… sometimes, yeah”

…it creates reflection.

👉 Aim for:

recognition, not agreement

7. 🗣️ “Tone of certainty”

High certainty = high threat

Dial it down slightly with:
“it seems”
“in my experience”
“I’ve noticed”

Not everywhere — just enough to signal:

“this is a perspective, not a decree”

8. 💥 “Where is the emotional spike?”

Every post has one or two lines that carry the sting.

Find them.

Then ask:

Do I want this to provoke thought… or defensiveness?

👉 You’ve reduced most of these already.

9. 🧍 “Have I included myself in the system?”

This is your ultimate shield.

Even one line like:

“I’ve definitely fallen into this myself”

turns the piece from:
❌ critique
➡️
✅ shared experience

10. 🎯 Final gut check

Before publishing, ask:

“If my boss read this, could I calmly stand behind every sentence?”

If the answer is yes:
→ you’re good

If there’s a hesitation:
→ that’s the sentence to soften
